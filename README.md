# Vendor Performance & Sales Analysis Report

1. Executive Summary
This report evaluates inventory, sales, and vendor performance using the vendor_sales_summary dataset. The analysis highlights key revenue drivers, supplier dependencies, and cost-efficiency trends. By understanding these dynamics, the business can streamline purchasing strategies, optimize inventory turnover, and maximize gross profit margins.

2. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Based on the overarching dataset (10,692 items), the following KPIs provide a baseline for business health:

Median Profit Margin: 30.40% (indicates a healthy and consistent baseline profitability across the majority of inventory items).

Average Stock Turnover: 1.70 (the average rate at which inventory is sold and replaced over the period).

Average Sales-to-Purchase Ratio: 2.50 (indicates strong revenue generation relative to purchasing costs).

Top 10 Vendor Sales Contribution: 65.69% (shows a heavy concentration of revenue tied to a select few suppliers).

3. Key Business Insights
A. Vendor Concentration & Market Leaders
A small fraction of vendors dictates the majority of sales volume. The Top 10 vendors account for roughly two-thirds (65.69%) of the total sales contribution.

Diageo North America Inc. is the undisputed top supplier, generating $67.99 million in sales (16.30% of total revenue) and yielding a massive gross profit of $17.89 million.

Martignetti Companies and Pernod Ricard USA follow closely, contributing $39.33 million (8.30%) and $32.06 million (7.76%) in sales, respectively.

B. Top Performing Brands
Specific flagship brands act as core revenue anchors for the business. The top five brands by total sales dollars are:

Jack Daniels No 7 Black – $7.96 million

Tito's Handmade Vodka – $7.40 million

Grey Goose Vodka – $7.21 million

Capt Morgan Spiced Rum – $6.35 million

Absolut 80 Proof – $6.24 million

C. Economies of Scale in Purchasing
The analysis answers a critical question regarding bulk purchasing: Does buying in bulk reduce the unit price? By categorizing total purchase quantities into terciles (Small, Medium, Large), a drastic reduction in cost is revealed:

Small Orders: Average Unit Purchase Price = $39.07

Medium Orders: Average Unit Purchase Price = $15.49

Large Orders: Average Unit Purchase Price = $10.78

Insight: Moving from "Small" to "Large" purchasing volumes reduces the average unit price by nearly 72%, highlighting massive economies of scale.

4. Actionable Recommendations
Capitalize on Volume Discounts: The data proves that bulk purchasing significantly drives down unit costs. Procurement teams should identify high-turnover items currently being ordered in "Small" or "Medium" batches and shift them to "Large" order sizes to secure the $10.78 average unit price.

Renegotiate with Tier-1 Vendors: Because Diageo, Martignetti, and Pernod Ricard account for over 32% of your total sales, the business has significant volume leverage. Use this to negotiate better payment terms, deeper bulk discounts, or freight cost subsidies with these specific suppliers.

Protect Core Inventory: Implement stringent minimum-stock alerts for the top-selling brands (Jack Daniels, Tito's, Grey Goose, etc.). Because these are high-velocity revenue drivers, any stockouts on these specific items will disproportionately impact the bottom line.

Consolidate the "Long Tail" of Vendors: With ~66% of sales coming from just 10 suppliers, the remaining hundreds of vendors contribute only ~34%. Review the performance of this "long tail." Cutting ties with consistently high-cost, low-turnover vendors will simplify supply chain logistics and reduce administrative overhead.
